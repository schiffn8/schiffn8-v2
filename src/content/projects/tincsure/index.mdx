## Context

Tincsure is a medicinal mushroom tincture brand. The packaging brief was to carve out space in a crowded supplement market without defaulting to the "earthy neutrals + serif" aesthetic that most wellness brands reach for first.

Three SKUs: Cordyceps, Turkey Tail, Lion's Mane. Each targets a different outcome — energy, immunity, cognition. The system needed to unify them as a family while making each SKU immediately legible at shelf distance.

## Approach

### The Pointillist System

The visual foundation is Pointillist illustration — individual dots accumulating into form, directly mirroring how the product works: small consistent doses composing into sustained effect over time. Each SKU gets its own illustration depicting the mushroom species. Dense enough to read as texture from a distance. Legible as art up close.

This gave the brand a single aesthetic decision that does all the work: color differentiation between SKUs, a unifying production technique, and a narrative that's legible without ever needing copy to explain it.

### Packaging System

Three SKUs, one set of structural decisions. Each box holds a tincture bottle, dosing card, and product insert. The color palette shifts between SKUs via hue, not value — the system reads as a family on shelf, not three unrelated products.

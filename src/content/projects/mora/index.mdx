## Context

The Museum of Russian Art (MORA) is an independent institution in Minneapolis — one of the few museums in the United States dedicated to Russian art spanning Muscovite Russia through the Soviet era and its successor states.

The institution's mission is serious and the collection is significant. Its identity wasn't. The rebrand brief was to design a visual system — and then a digital product — that let the work lead, not the branding.

This project covered the full stack: identity system through case study through interactive prototype. Both were completed as coursework; the UX component was developed using the Google UX Design Certificate framework.

## Brand Identity

### Typography

The identity anchors on typography. MORA — the acronym — is set as a wordmark that doubles as a brandmark: the letterforms are chosen and spaced so that the full name and the single initial work as independent marks at any scale.

### Poster System

The poster series applies the identity to programming communications. Full-bleed photography, minimal type, unbroken editorial grid. The goal was a visual language that a serious arts institution would actually use — not a museum that looks designed to look like a museum.

Coursework — brand and UX developed as linked projects in the same year. The dual-track nature of the project (identity → digital product) was intentional: a museum identity that only lives on paper doesn't solve the right problem.

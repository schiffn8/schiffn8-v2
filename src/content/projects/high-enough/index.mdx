## Context

"High Enough?" is a data visualization editorial piece on the scale of America's opioid and fentanyl epidemic. It came before [*The Fix*](/work/the-fix) — the research and visual language developed here became the foundation for that project.

The design problem with public health data is that the numbers are too large to feel real. 80,000 overdose deaths in a year is a statistic. It lands differently as a visual argument. The editorial goal was to find a graphic language that made the scale visceral — naturalistic rather than clinical, impossible to skim past.

The title is a rhetorical question aimed directly at the viewer: at what point is the death toll high enough to change behavior? It's not answered. The data is the answer.

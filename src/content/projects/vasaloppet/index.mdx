## Context

Vasaloppet USA is a Nordic ski park in Mora, Minnesota — named after Sweden's most famous cross-country ski race, the Vasaloppet, which has run annually since 1922. The park hosts year-round events, including a winter triathlon combining running, biking, and skiing.

The existing event branding leaned on generic alpine conventions. The speculative brief was to build an identity that earned its Swedish heritage rather than decorating with it — specific to this place, this race, this cultural lineage.

## Approach

### Heritage as System, Not Decoration

The identity draws from two sources: the Dalahäst — the iconic Swedish carved horse — and the horizontal sweep of a Nordic winter landscape at dawn. Neither appears literally. The Dalahäst informs the weight and stance of the letterforms; the landscape becomes the proportional logic of the layout system.

The color palette is cold: deep navy, ice white, a single warm accent that references the painted horse's traditional red without reproducing it.

## Outcome

Speculative coursework. The project explored how to design an identity that earns cultural specificity — grounding visual decisions in the actual history and geography of a place rather than borrowing generic signifiers.
